# Mitacs Accelerate — Application Brief
### En Pensent: Photonic Pattern Recognition R&D Internships

**Program:** Mitacs Accelerate
**URL:** https://www.mitacs.ca/our-programs/accelerate-researchers-and-postdocs/
**Funding:** $15,000 per 4-month internship unit (Mitacs $7,500 + En Pensent $7,500)
**Stackable:** Up to 16 units per intern (2+ years), multiple interns simultaneously
**Eligibility:** Canadian company partnered with a Canadian university
**Timeline:** Rolling applications, year-round

---

## 1. PROGRAM OVERVIEW

Mitacs Accelerate connects Canadian companies with graduate students and postdoctoral fellows for applied R&D projects. Each 4-month internship unit costs En Pensent $7,500, with Mitacs matching $7,500 — giving you a $15,000 researcher for $7,500.

**Key advantages:**
- 50% cost subsidy on skilled R&D labour
- Access to university lab equipment and expertise
- Intern can work on-site at En Pensent or at the university
- No equity dilution — this is a grant, not investment
- Builds academic publication pipeline (strengthens future grant applications)
- Can lead to full-time hire after internship

## 2. INTERN PROJECT PROPOSALS

### Project A: Photonic Chip Layout Design (1 intern, 8-12 months)

**University department:** Electrical/Computer Engineering — Photonics/Integrated Optics group

**Intern profile:** PhD student or postdoc in silicon photonics, integrated photonic circuits, or optical computing

**Scope:**
- Translate the universal grid software architecture into a photonic circuit layout
- Design micro-ring resonator array (12 neurons, 27 channels) in SOI PDK
- Simulate optical interference patterns and verify accuracy matches software baseline
- Design thermo-optic phase shifters for self-learning weight updates
- Produce fabrication-ready GDS layout file

**Deliverables:**
- Photonic circuit schematic and layout (GDS-II format)
- Simulation results: accuracy, power budget, insertion loss, crosstalk
- Fabrication readiness report
- Co-authored publication

**Units:** 2-3 units (8-12 months)
**En Pensent cost:** $15,000–$22,500
**Total value:** $30,000–$45,000

### Project B: New Domain Adapters & Cross-Domain Transfer (1 intern, 8 months)

**University department:** Computer Science — Machine Learning, Signal Processing, or Data Science

**Intern profile:** MSc or PhD student in machine learning, signal processing, or applied math

**Scope:**
- Develop 3 new domain adapters for the universal grid architecture:
  - Medical: ECG/EEG temporal patterns → grid encoding
  - Seismic: Vibration sensor arrays → grid mapping
  - Industrial: Manufacturing sensor streams → fault detection
- Benchmark each adapter on public datasets
- Measure cross-domain transfer: does training on one domain improve others?
- Statistical analysis of self-learning convergence across domains

**Deliverables:**
- 3 validated domain adapters with benchmark results
- Cross-domain transfer analysis report
- Publication: "Universal Temporal Pattern Recognition Across N Domains"

**Units:** 2 units (8 months)
**En Pensent cost:** $15,000
**Total value:** $30,000

### Project C: Defence Sensor Fusion Benchmarking (1 intern, 8 months)

**University department:** Electrical Engineering — Radar/Signals, or Mechanical Engineering — Acoustics

**Intern profile:** MSc or PhD student in radar signal processing, sonar, or sensor fusion

**Scope:**
- Build radar domain adapter (range-Doppler → grid encoding)
- Build acoustic/sonar domain adapter (spectrogram → grid mapping)
- Benchmark multi-sensor fusion on unclassified defence-relevant datasets
- Compare against traditional Bayesian and Dempster-Shafer fusion baselines
- Document results for IDEaS defence grant applications

**Deliverables:**
- Radar and acoustic domain adapters
- Fusion benchmark report (vs. traditional methods)
- IDEaS-ready technical documentation
- Publication: "Photonic Interference-Based Multi-Sensor Fusion"

**Units:** 2 units (8 months)
**En Pensent cost:** $15,000
**Total value:** $30,000

### Project D: Energy Efficiency & Hardware Benchmarking (1 intern, 4-8 months)

**University department:** Electrical/Computer Engineering — Computer Architecture or VLSI

**Intern profile:** MSc or PhD student in computer architecture, energy-efficient computing, or VLSI

**Scope:**
- Rigorous power/energy measurement of universal grid software on various hardware
- Model projected photonic chip power consumption based on circuit design
- Compare: GPU (A100), TPU, FPGA, ASIC, photonic chip — energy per inference
- Build energy efficiency comparison dataset for grant applications and publications

**Deliverables:**
- Energy benchmarking report across hardware platforms
- Photonic chip power model with validation
- Publication: "5000× Energy Reduction: Photonic vs. Electronic Pattern Recognition"

**Units:** 1-2 units (4-8 months)
**En Pensent cost:** $7,500–$15,000
**Total value:** $15,000–$30,000

## 4. HOW TO APPLY

1. **Identify a university partner** — contact a professor whose research aligns with one of the projects above
2. **Professor identifies intern** — from their existing graduate students or recruits one
3. **Joint application** — En Pensent and professor submit together on Mitacs portal
4. **Review:** 6-8 weeks
5. **Internship starts** upon approval

### Application Requirements
- Project description (2-3 pages)
- Intern CV
- Supervisor (professor) letter of support
- En Pensent commitment letter ($7,500/unit)
- Expected outcomes and deliverables
