## WHEN YOUR ITA CALLS BACK — KEY TALKING POINTS

### The 30-Second Pitch

"We've built a pattern recognition system that works identically across completely different domains — chess games, battery health, and chemical sensors — using the same algorithm. No other AI system does this. The architecture is designed for photonic hardware: a chip that processes data using light instead of electricity, running 5,000 times more efficiently than a GPU. We have working software proof on real data, and we want IRAP funding to build the photonic chip."

### If They Ask: "What makes this different from other AI?"

"Three things:
1. **Universal** — same algorithm works on chess, batteries, and chemical sensors. We don't retrain for each domain.
2. **Self-learning** — it gets better automatically as it sees more data. No human tuning.
3. **Photonic** — designed from the ground up for light-based computing. Less than 10 milliwatts versus 300 watts for a GPU. That's 30,000 times more efficient."

### If They Ask: "Do you have proof it works?"

"Yes, validated on three real-world datasets:
- **Chess:** 54.4% next-move prediction on 50,000+ real games. Random chance is about 4%. Statistical significance is beyond any doubt.
- **Battery health:** 89% detection of critical battery degradation events, tested on NASA's own dataset — 140 battery cells, 114,000 charge cycles.
- **Chemical fault detection:** F1 score of 93.3% on the Tennessee Eastman benchmark, which is the gold standard in process engineering. That's 20 points better than the established baseline.

All three use the identical algorithm. Only the input encoding changes."

### If They Ask: "What's the market?"

"Three immediate markets:
1. **Defence** — Canada's IDEaS program wants multi-sensor fusion. Our chip fuses radar, sonar, and IR simultaneously in under 1 nanosecond. Direct fit for the Canadian Surface Combatant and NORAD modernization.
2. **Energy** — Battery health monitoring for EV fleets, grid storage, and aerospace. We've already proven it on NASA's battery data.
3. **Industrial** — Chemical process monitoring, predictive maintenance. Our 93.3% F1 on Tennessee Eastman outperforms existing solutions.

Total addressable market for photonic AI chips: estimated $50B+ by 2030."

### If They Ask: "What do you need funding for?"

"Two things:
1. **Photonic chip design** — Translate our validated software into a silicon photonics circuit layout. We need to hire or contract photonic design engineers and access simulation tools.
2. **Prototype fabrication** — Tape out a first chip through CMC Microsystems or AIM Photonics. This requires foundry access and testing equipment.

We're looking at $500K–$1M over 18-24 months. The outcome is a fabricated photonic chip that performs the same pattern recognition we've proven in software."

### If They Ask: "Do you have university partners?"

"We're actively reaching out to Canadian photonics labs — Université Laval COPL, University of Waterloo, UBC, and University of Toronto. We're pursuing NSERC Alliance and Mitacs partnerships in parallel. We've also applied to MIT Startup Exchange for international collaboration.

A university partner would handle the photonic circuit design research while we provide the algorithm, datasets, and domain expertise."

### If They Ask: "Are you claiming SR&ED?"

"We're in the process of setting that up. All of our R&D work — the universal grid architecture, self-learning algorithms, cross-domain benchmarking, and photonic chip design work — should qualify. We're planning to engage an SR&ED consultant."

### If They Ask: "What's your IP situation?"

"We have a patent pending on the core architecture — the Synaptic Truth Network. The universal grid portal, self-learning mechanism, and cross-domain transfer approach are all covered. Canadian-owned IP."

## AFTER THE ITA MEETING

Your ITA will either:
1. **Help you write the IRAP application** — this is the best case. They guide you through it.
2. **Suggest other NRC programs** — IRAP has several streams, they'll match you to the right one.
3. **Connect you to resources** — other programs, mentors, or NRC labs.

**Key: The ITA is your advocate inside NRC.** Be open, honest about where you are (software proof, no hardware yet), and clear about what you need. They've funded hundreds of tech startups — your story is compelling.
