## 3. PROJECT DESCRIPTION

### Background

En Pensent has developed a universal photonic pattern recognition architecture validated on 3 domains:
- **Chess:** 59.7% next-move prediction (p ≈ 0, z > 37) on 50K+ real games
- **Battery:** 56.5% accuracy, 89% critical detection on NASA MATR dataset (140 cells, 114K cycles)
- **Chemical:** F1 93.3% (+20.6pp over baseline) on Tennessee Eastman benchmark (52 sensors)

The architecture runs as software today. The next step is **photonic hardware** — translating the algorithm into a silicon photonics chip that performs inference at <1ns latency and <10mW power.

### Why MIT

MIT has the world's leading silicon photonics groups:
- **Prof. Marin Soljačić:** Published >96% accuracy integrated photonic processor (Dec 2024)
- **Prof. Dirk Englund:** Quantum photonics, programmable photonic circuits
- **Prof. Jelena Notaros:** Integrated photonics, free-space optical coupling

MIT also has direct access to **AIM Photonics** (Albany, NY) — America's silicon photonics foundry, offering Multi-Project Wafer runs for prototyping.

### Collaboration Goals

1. **Technical review:** MIT faculty evaluate En Pensent's architecture for photonic implementation feasibility
2. **Co-design:** Joint design of photonic circuit layout in SOI (Silicon-on-Insulator) PDK
3. **Fabrication:** First prototype tape-out at AIM Photonics via MIT partnership
4. **Publication:** Co-authored paper establishing the photonic implementation pathway
5. **Grant leverage:** Joint MIT-En Pensent applications to DARPA, ARPA-E, and other US programs
